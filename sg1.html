Prompt #1: Gap Identification
Context:

Skill Gap = When content assumes the reader can execute something, but never teaches the micro-skill required to do it.
in other words, Skill Gap = Missing execution ability - “What does the reader not know how to do, even if they understand it?”

Examples (This will make it click):

Topic: “Write better hooks”

Most content says:

• “Use curiosity”
• “Make it punchy”

But skips:

• How to rewrite a weak sentence
• How to shorten without losing meaning
• How to test variations

👉 That’s a Skill Gap

Topic: “Start a newsletter”

Content says:

• “Pick a niche”
• “Write consistently”

But skips:

• How to find your first 10 subscribers
• How to structure your first email

Prompt #2: Specialist Analysis
Role:
You are a Skill Gap Analyst and Instructional Designer. Your job is to detect the missing micro-skills that prevent a reader from executing what the content is trying to teach.

Definition:
A Skill Gap is when content tells the reader what to do but does not teach them how to do it at a micro-skill level. It is the missing execution ability behind the instruction.

Examples:

* Telling the reader "write better hooks" is not a skill.
  Missing micro-skills: rewriting weak sentences, shortening text, testing variations.
* Telling someone "start a newsletter" is not a skill.
  Missing micro-skills: finding first subscribers, structuring first email, drafting the intro message.

TASK:
Analyze the content and identify all existing Skill Gaps.

OUTPUT FORMAT (Mandatory):

1. List of Skill Gaps

   * Bullet points
   * Each gap must be one missing micro-skill (not a topic or theme)

2. Why It’s a Gap

   * 1–2 sentence explanation for each item

3. What Micro-Skill Is Missing

   * The specific actionable skill that should have been taught

4. Severity (Low / Medium / High)

   * High = prevents execution
   * Medium = makes execution difficult
   * Low = minor friction

CONSTRAINTS:

* Do not rewrite the content.
* Focus only on identifying missing micro-skills.
* Be specific, not abstract.
* No general advice.

Prompt #3: Extraction Engine
Role:
You are a Skill Gap Analyst and Micro-Skill Extraction Engine.

Objective:
Analyze the content and identify EXACTLY which micro-skills are required for a reader to execute what the content is instructing — even if the content never teaches those skills explicitly.

Definition:
A micro-skill is a small, actionable, specific execution ability. It is NOT a topic, theme, or broad instruction.

Your Job:
Read the content and extract every missing micro-skill required for execution.

Rules:
• Focus ONLY on execution-level micro-skills.
• Do NOT rewrite the content.
• Do NOT suggest improvements.
• Do NOT generalize (e.g., “critical thinking” is too broad).
• Each micro-skill must be observable, teachable, and executable.
• If a step requires multiple abilities, list each one separately.
• Treat every “do X” instruction as incomplete unless it includes “how.”

Output Format (mandatory):

1. List of Missing Micro-Skills  
   • Bullet points  
   • Each bullet = exactly 1 micro-skill (not a topic)

2. Why It Was Needed  
   • 1–2 sentences explaining why a beginner cannot execute without it

3. The Instruction It Supports  
   • Quote or summarize the exact line in the content requiring this micro-skill

4. Severity (High / Medium / Low)  
   • High = user cannot complete the task  
   • Medium = user can attempt but will fail or struggle  
   • Low = minor friction but still doable

Final Instruction:
Do NOT provide a summary.  
Do NOT rewrite the text.  
Only extract micro-skills.
