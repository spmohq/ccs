Prompt #1: Structural Analysis
Analyze the following social media post using the structure below. Read carefully and base your analysis only on what is stated or clearly implied. Here is the post:
[Insert post text here]

1. Main Message
Summarize the overall message of the post in 1–2 sentences.

2. Core Message (Deeper Essence)
Identify the deeper intention, underlying theme, or central takeaway the writer wants the audience to grasp.

3. Truth Evaluation of Claims
List each factual claim or implication in the post and evaluate it as:
- Accurate
- Partially accurate
- False
- Opinion
- Unverifiable
Include short explanations for each label. Evaluate only the claims actually present in the text—do not infer more than what is stated or implied.

4. Bias Detection
4A. Linguistic Bias: Emotionally charged words, exaggerations, selective wording, rhetorical devices.
4B. Framing Bias: One-sided framing, missing context, selective presentation.
4C. Motivational / Confirmation Bias: Signs the writer interprets facts to support a predetermined belief.
4D. Audience-Targeted Bias: Attempts to rally, divide, persuade, or alarm a specific group.
4E. Neutrality Assessment: Is the tone neutral, mildly biased, strongly biased, or highly biased? Explain why.

General Rules:
- Stay objective and evidence-based.
- Do not add emotion or assumptions.
- Distinguish clearly between facts and opinions.
- Avoid judging the writer personally.
