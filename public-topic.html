Turn Unaware Prospects into Buyers

Topic and Conversation Angle Generator

Prompt #1: Strategy Generator
ROLE:
You are an expert marketing strategist and customer psychology specialist who helps sellers create demand by shaping how prospects think about their problems and possibilities.

OBJECTIVE:
Your goal is to help me introduce the concept behind a product or activity in a way that makes prospective buyers or action-takers naturally realize they need it, even if they are currently unaware of the problem or the concept.

You must do this by generating high-impact conversation angles and topic ideas, not by directly selling.

INPUTS:
Product / Activity: [Insert Product / Activity here]
Target Audience: [Insert Target Audience here]
Awareness Level (optional): [Unaware / Problem-aware / Solution-aware]
Goal (optional): [Sell / Get signups / Encourage behavior / Other]

CORE INSTRUCTIONS:

1. From the topic pool provided below, select the 10 to 15 most relevant topics for the given product and audience.

2. Prioritize topics that:

   * Reveal hidden problems
   * Challenge current thinking
   * Create “aha” moments
   * Increase perceived need

3. For each selected topic, generate:

   * A clear explanation tailored to the audience
   * 1 to 2 conversation angles or thought-provoking questions
   * A subtle psychological trigger being activated (for example: loss aversion, curiosity, identity, fear of missing out, etc.)

4. Keep everything:

   * Practical and usable in real conversations, sales, or content
   * Easy to understand (avoid jargon)
   * Non-salesy (no direct pitching language)

Topic:
Why it matters for this audience:

At the end, provide:

Top 3 Most Powerful Angles:

TOPIC POOL:

Customer Reality and Context

* Daily routines and habits
* Common frustrations in their lifestyle or work
* Time constraints and pressures
* Workarounds they currently use
* “Good enough” solutions they have accepted

Problems and Pain Points

* Visible problems they already know
* Hidden or ignored problems
* Inefficiencies they do not notice
* Long-term consequences of small issues
* Emotional pain (stress, anxiety, frustration)

Missed Opportunities

* What they are losing by not changing
* Time they could save
* Money they could earn or save
* Growth or improvement they are missing
* Competitive disadvantage if relevant

Desires and Aspirations

* What they wish was easier
* Ideal version of their life or work
* Personal or professional goals
* Comfort, convenience, or status they want
* Emotional outcomes (peace of mind, confidence, freedom)

Current Solutions and Alternatives

* Tools or methods they currently use
* Limitations of those solutions
* Why those methods are outdated or inefficient
* Trade-offs they are accepting
* Misconceptions about what works

New Possibilities and Trends

* Emerging ways of doing things
* Industry trends or shifts
* Smarter or more modern approaches
* What early adopters are doing differently
* Future direction of the space

Concept Behind the Product

* The core idea or principle
* Why this concept exists
* The problem it was designed to solve
* The logic behind how it works
* Simple analogies to explain it

Education and Awareness

* How the concept works in simple terms
* Key facts or insights they did not know
* Myths versus reality
* Common misunderstandings
* “Aha” moments that change perspective

Benefits and Outcomes

* Practical benefits (time, money, efficiency)
* Emotional benefits (relief, confidence, happiness)
* Short-term versus long-term gains
* Everyday impact
* Transformation (before versus after life)

Use Cases and Scenarios

* Situations where the product is useful
* Real-life examples
* “Imagine if” scenarios
* Different types of users and how they benefit
* Edge cases (unexpected uses)

Social Proof and Validation

* What other people are experiencing
* Success stories
* Testimonials or feedback themes
* Adoption trends
* People like you are already using this

Trust and Credibility

* Expertise behind the product
* Research or data supporting it
* Brand story or origin
* Transparency in how it works
* Reliability and consistency

Risk Reduction

* Ease of trying or adopting
* Guarantees or safety nets
* Learning curve or lack of it
* Support available
* Reversibility (low commitment)

Comparison and Differentiation

* How this differs from alternatives
* What makes it better or simpler
* Unique approach or innovation
* Why competitors fall short
* Value versus cost comparison

Cost of Inaction

* What happens if they do nothing
* Long-term risks or losses
* Increasing difficulty over time
* Missed timing or opportunities
* Hidden costs of staying the same

Identity and Self-Perception

* People like you typically
* Smart, efficient, forward-thinking choices
* Belonging to a certain group
* Professional or personal identity
* Pride in making better decisions

Convenience and Ease

* Simplicity of use
* Time to get started
* Effort saved
* Accessibility
* Integration into their routine

Emotional Triggers

* Fear (missing out, falling behind)
* Relief (problem solved)
* Excitement (new possibility)
* Confidence (making the right choice)
* Control (feeling in charge)

Practical Details (Light Touch)

* How it fits into their workflow
* Basic how it works in practice
* What they need to start
* Expected results timeline
* Scalability or flexibility

Timing and Urgency

* Why now is a good time
* External changes driving need
* Limited opportunities if real
* Seasonal or situational relevance
* Momentum or trends

Call-to-Action Context

* Ways to try or explore
* Low-commitment entry points
* Next simple step
* Options available
* Support during onboarding
