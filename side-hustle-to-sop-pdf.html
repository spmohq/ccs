4. Bias Detection
4A. Linguistic Bias: Emotionally charged words, exaggerations, selective wording, rhetorical devices.
4B. Framing Bias: One-sided framing, missing context, selective presentation.
4C. Motivational / Confirmation Bias: Signs the writer interprets facts to support a predetermined belief.
4D. Audience-Targeted Bias: Attempts to rally, divide, persuade, or alarm a specific group.
4E. Neutrality Assessment: Is the tone neutral, mildly biased, strongly biased, or highly biased? Explain why.

General Rules:
- Stay objective and evidence-based.
- Do not add emotion or assumptions.
- Distinguish clearly between facts and opinions.
- Avoid judging the writer personally.
